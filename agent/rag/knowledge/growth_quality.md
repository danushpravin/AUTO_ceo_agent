# Growth Quality — Thresholds and Interpretation Framework

## What Is Growth Quality

Revenue growth is only valuable if it is profitable. Growth quality measures whether increasing revenue is creating real business value or merely disguising structural deterioration. A business can show strong top-line growth while simultaneously destroying equity through loss-making products, unsustainable pricing, or cost inflation. Growth quality analysis separates real expansion from accounting illusions.

## Core Signal: Revenue Delta vs Profit Reality

The primary growth quality check compares the direction of revenue change against the profitability of the products driving that change. A positive revenue delta (month-over-month or recent period vs baseline) is only a healthy signal if total profit is also positive. When revenue grows but aggregate profit is negative or near zero, the business is expanding its losses, not its value. This is the most dangerous form of fake growth because it appears healthy on dashboards while the underlying economics deteriorate.

Threshold: If total profit is zero or negative while revenue delta is positive, classify the growth signal as NEGATIVE with HIGH confidence. This situation requires immediate investigation into cost structure and pricing.

## Loss Revenue Share Threshold

Even when overall profit is positive, growth can be structurally compromised if a significant portion of revenue is generated by loss-making products. These products consume working capital, marketing spend, and operational resources while destroying margin. The threshold for concern is when loss-making products contribute more than 30% of total revenue. Above this level, the profitable segment of the business is effectively subsidising the unprofitable segment, and growth in revenue is misleading.

Threshold: Loss revenue share above 0.30 (30%) with positive revenue delta signals NEGATIVE growth quality at HIGH confidence. Between 15% and 30% warrants a CAUTION signal. Below 15% is acceptable if overall profit is positive.

## Interpreting Growth as POSITIVE

Growth can be classified as genuinely positive only when three conditions are met simultaneously: revenue delta is positive, total profit across the portfolio is positive, and loss-making products contribute less than 30% of revenue. When all three hold, the business is growing its profitable core. This does not mean risk is absent — concentration risk, channel dependency, or inventory fragility may still exist — but the growth itself is real.

## Neutral Growth Signal

When revenue delta is near zero, negative, or not statistically meaningful, the growth signal is neutral. This is not inherently alarming. Flat revenue with improving margins is often healthier than revenue growth with deteriorating margins. A NEUTRAL signal means growth quality cannot be assessed and other dimensions (profitability, efficiency, inventory) should drive the executive analysis.

## Common Fake Growth Patterns to Flag

Fake growth appears in several recurring patterns. Volume-driven growth with price erosion occurs when units sold increase but average selling price falls, producing revenue growth that cannot sustain margins. Promotional growth occurs when discounting temporarily inflates revenue but trains customers to wait for sales, destroying baseline demand. Loss-leader scaling occurs when a single loss-making product is aggressively pushed to grow revenue metrics, inflating top-line numbers while the portfolio bleeds. Channel-subsidised growth occurs when a high-spend marketing channel is buying revenue at a cost that exceeds the margin generated. All of these patterns share the same signature: revenue goes up, profit does not follow.

## Confidence Levels

HIGH confidence signals are generated when the data is complete and the directional evidence is unambiguous — profit clearly negative with growing revenue, or loss revenue share clearly above threshold. MEDIUM confidence applies when data is partially missing or the margin between observed values and thresholds is small. LOW confidence applies when insufficient data exists to make any directional call.
